01 — Overview

What is it?

A B2B SaaS platform built for restaurant owners who need help managing their online presence. The platform handles Google Business Profile management, commission-free online ordering, website creation, and automated email marketing — all from a single dashboard.

Restaurants pay a monthly subscription and get a dedicated team (powered by software automation) managing their listings, responding to reviews, keeping menus in sync across platforms, and driving repeat visits. The goal: let restaurant owners focus on food, not on fighting with Google.

02 — The Problem

Restaurants are invisible online

Most customers discover restaurants through Google. But the average local restaurant has an outdated Google Business Profile, unanswered reviews, menus that don't match reality, and no website beyond a basic Facebook page.

Third-party ordering platforms like DoorDash and Toast charge 20-30% commissions on every order. Restaurant owners know they're leaving money on the table, but they don't have time to manage yet another platform between running a kitchen, managing staff, and keeping the doors open.

"Most restaurant owners I talked to don't lack ambition — they lack hours in the day."
03 — Genesis

Walking the DFW strip

The idea came from noticing how many local restaurants in the Dallas-Fort Worth area had outdated Google listings — wrong hours, old menus, zero review responses. These weren't failing restaurants. They were busy restaurants with no bandwidth for digital.

I started mapping the problem: restaurants pay commission-heavy platforms because they seem easy, but they're actually expensive. A managed service that keeps their Google presence accurate and captures orders directly could save them thousands per month — and it could be mostly automated with the right software.

05 — Architecture

Three apps in one

The platform is a multi-tenant application with three distinct interfaces serving different user types — all built on a shared data layer.

01

Marketing Site

Public-facing pages with pricing, competitor comparisons, social proof, and feature breakdowns. Designed to convert restaurant owners browsing for solutions.

02

Restaurant Portal

Customer dashboard showing service milestones, recent activity, and a request system for menu changes, hours updates, and support. Restaurants see their progress and stay informed.

03

Admin Dashboard

Internal CRM with full client management, sales pipeline, onboarding workflows, support queue, activity logs, and integration credential management.

04

Shared Data Layer

Convex real-time backend with 15+ data models powering client lifecycle tracking, milestone-based onboarding, health scoring, and complete audit trails.

07 — Data & Workflows

Real business logic

This isn't a toy project — the data model tracks the full lifecycle of a restaurant client from first contact to active service:

Client Lifecycle
Lead → Onboarding → Active → Paused → Churned — with health scoring (green/yellow/red) and churn risk tracking
Sales Pipeline
Inquiry → Demo Scheduled → Demo Completed → Proposal → Negotiating → Won/Lost — full CRM for tracking leads through conversion
Onboarding
Milestone-based: GBP Live → Ordering Live → Website Live → Marketing Live — progress visible to both admin and client
Integrations
Google Business Profile, GloriaFood, Stripe, Domain Providers, Email Services — credentials stored securely per-client
08 — Design Philosophy

Dark-first, built for kitchens

The design system is intentionally dark-mode first — optimized for restaurant operators checking their dashboard late at night or in dimly lit kitchens. The color palette draws from culinary and hospitality tones: mesquite greens, burnt chili reds, warm brass accents.

Every interface is designed for skim-reading. Restaurant owners don't have time to parse dense dashboards. The UI prioritizes clarity, contrast, and the ability to get the information you need in seconds.

09 — What's Next
