01 — Overview

What is it?

A computer vision pipeline that analyzes industrial manufacturing footage to generate discrete event timing data. The system tracks stamp machines, auto loaders, forklifts, and human operators across factory floor video, extracting precise timing and sequence information.

This structured data feeds directly into Simio simulation software, enabling manufacturing clients to model and optimize their throughput without weeks of manual observation and stopwatch timing.

02 — The Problem

Manual data collection doesn't scale

Discrete event simulation is one of the most powerful tools for optimizing manufacturing throughput. But it requires accurate timing data — how long each machine cycle takes, how often operators intervene, how material flows between stations.

Traditionally, this data is collected by engineers standing on the floor with clipboards and stopwatches. It's slow, error-prone, limited in sample size, and can't capture the full picture of complex multi-station workflows. Existing camera infrastructure was going unused.

"You can't optimize what you can't measure — and manual measurement misses more than it catches."
03 — Genesis

Camera infrastructure, untapped

The insight was straightforward: manufacturing facilities already have extensive camera coverage for security purposes. That same footage contains all the timing data needed for simulation — it just needs to be extracted computationally.

Developed at Worlds for an industrial manufacturing client, this project turned an existing investment (security cameras) into a continuous source of high-fidelity operational data.

04 — The Pipeline

From footage to simulation

01

Video Ingestion

Raw footage from factory floor cameras is ingested and pre-processed — stabilization, frame normalization, and region-of-interest extraction.

02

Object Detection & Tracking

Computer vision models identify and track machines, vehicles (forklifts), and operators across frames, maintaining identity through occlusions and camera transitions.

03

Event Recognition

State changes are detected — machine cycle starts/stops, loader operations, operator interventions — converting continuous video into discrete events with precise timestamps.

04

Time-Series Storage

Event data streams into InfluxDB, providing a queryable time-series database of all manufacturing activities with sub-second precision.

05

Visualization & Export

Dash/Plotly dashboards provide real-time and historical views. Structured data exports feed directly into Simio for discrete event simulation.
